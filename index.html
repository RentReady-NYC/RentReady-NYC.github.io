The Turnover Chain Reaction

Abandoned furniture paralyzes the turnover process. Until the unit is empty, cleaners can't scrub, painters can't prep, and brokers can't show the space.

In walk-up buildings, removing items safely requires labor and coordination that stretches your team thin. Storing items inside the unit exposes you to further delays and legal risk if possession timelines aren't strictly managed.

Example Cost of Delay

A 5-day delay waiting to clear an apartment renting for $3,600/month means $600 in lost rental income. This easily outweighs the resale value of leftover items.

Your goals: reduced vacancy days, reduced legal exposure, reduced vendor uncertainty, and reduced staff strain.

Moving tenant property comes with liability. We mitigate this through a rigorous chain of custody. By documenting exactly what was left, in what condition, and where it was taken, we help you support legal holding requirements and reduce exposure to claims.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if the tenant comes back for items? +

Items are held safely in our facility. You simply authorize a release, and the tenant can retrieve their belongings directly from our storage location, keeping them off your property.

How do you document condition? +

Our crew takes before/after photos and timestamped images of all removed assets. We log any obvious pre-existing damage to furniture to protect you against false claims.

How fast can you arrive? +

We aim for same-day dispatch if contacted before 12 PM, or guaranteed next working day.

Do you handle walk-ups with narrow stairs? +

Yes. NYC walk-ups are our specialty. Our teams are trained in tight-clearance removal and always pad door frames.

How long do you store items? +

We strictly adhere to local compliance limits (most often 30 days of holding). After that window expires and we have your sign-off, we process the items.

What if something gets damaged? +

We carry comprehensive coverage for property damage. If we scrape a hallway wall moving a dresser, we repair it.
